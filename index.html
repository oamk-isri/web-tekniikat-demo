The Moving Stars

The moving stars, as the ancient greeks used to call them. Better known as the classical planets, they are the celestial objects of the sky that move over time and are visible to the naked eye.

Over the cource of history, they have been the object of fascination and study for humanity. Ancient civilizations all over the world studied, predicted, and made their own conjectures about these anomalies of the otherwise seeminly unchanging sky.

Of course, today we know the true nature of these celestial bodies better than ever. The previously divine perception of these objects has slowly been changing to the one of scientific wonder. However, the old beliefs permeate to the daily life of a modern person in many forms. For instance the concept of a seven day week, one day for each celestial body.
